Legal and ethical concerns within news production

In order for a newscast to remain impartial, present news objectively, and be true to the viewer, several considerations have to be made. here, I will cover a few.

In the US, filming people without their consent is legal as of the first amendment. News stations have to consider people's privacy for safety, even if it is perfectly legal to disregard.

The cost of production at news stations often runs high, so news stations are rarely ever non-profit; in order to make money, news statons take sponsorships and advertise. Advertisers can threaten to cut funding if the station airs anything that the advertiser doesnt want their name on, which causes the news station to loose some of their transparency.

Stages of news production

1. Planning

Planning is a broad catagory that includes processes like gathering news, brainsorming stories, fact checking, negotiating with advertizers, and general organization. What this often looks like is producers and directors making a rough outline of the newscast, complete with how it will be produced.

2. Production

Production is the stage where the outine made in the planning stage gets filmed, composited, and edited together.

3. Distribution

In the distribution stage, a newscast gets aired to the public via radio and satellite television, as well as online.
